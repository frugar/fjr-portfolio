The TabApp mobile game, released in 2013, combined an iOS game with physical collectible toys that would unlock content and playable character in the game. We developed a battle arena where players could control and combat over 100 hero and villians from MARVEL and DC Comics in variopus types of skirmish modes. In addition to providing art direction on the project, I visualized and created all of the special effects to bring life to the characters’ super powers, conceived and directed the playable maps for the battles, contributed pop-up comic style introductions to the various boss villains, and devised the user experience for the product.



In addition to developing the digital game, I also collaborated with a team of engineers in designing and prototyping the capacitive bases on which the physical HeroClix toys were mounted and the Clix Station that was featured in TabApp elite.​ This game was developed in collaboration with WizKids, MARVEL & DC Comics to ensure a digital experience that maintained the quality, spirit and value of their brands. It also had the worst product name in history, but no one would listen to me about that.
